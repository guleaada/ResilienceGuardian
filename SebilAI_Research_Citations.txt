CROP CONTEXT:
- Noug is INDIGENOUS to Ethiopia (center of origin)
- Domesticated before 3000 BC in Ethiopian highlands
- Ethiopia produces ~50% of world's Noug
- ~285,236 hectares cultivated in Ethiopia (40% of total oil
 crop area), ~159,820 tonnes/year (26% of total oilseed
 production)
- Grown in highlands 1600-2200 masl: Amhara, Oromia, SNNPR,
 Tigray
- Oil content 30-50%
- Three traditional Ethiopian varieties:
 • 'abat noug' (highland, long-season, June-December)
 • 'mesno noug' (short-season, waterlogged soils)
 • 'bunegne noug' (lowland)

KEY DISEASES (per Ethiopian research):
- Alternaria Leaf Spot (Alternaria alternata) — most common
- Cercospora Leaf Spot (Cercospora guizoticola)
- Alternaria Stem & Leaf Blight — devastating for
 early-maturing accessions in wet seasons
- Root Rot (Macrophomina phaseolina)
- Bacterial Blight (Pseudomonas spp.)
- Sclerotinia Wilt

KEY PEST:
- Niger Leaf Miner (Sphaeroderma guizotiae Selman) —
 indigenous Ethiopian pest, northwestern Ethiopia

CROP CONTEXT:
- Major Ethiopian vegetable cash crop
- ~9,768 ha annually, ~913,000 tonnes
- Main production: Central Rift Valley (Adama, Meki, Ziway)
- Melkassa ARC (8°24'N, 39°21'E, 1550 m.a.s.l.) is national
 tomato research authority
- Optimal: 21-24°C, well-drained soil
- Resistant varieties: Melkashola, Melkasalsa, Bisholla,
 Sirinka-1, Gelilema, ARP tomato d2, Roma VF

KEY DISEASES (per EIAR Melkassa 2024 Technical Manual):
1. Tuta absoluta (Tomato Leafminer) — invasive since 2012,
 80-100% loss potential. PRIORITY #1.
2. Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans) — 63.7-100% loss
3. Early Blight (Alternaria solani)
4. Bacterial Wilt (Ralstonia solanacearum)
5. Powdery Mildew
6. Fusarium Wilt
7. Bacterial Spot

KEY PESTS:
1. Tuta absoluta (priority)
2. Whiteflies
3. African Bollworm
4. Spider Mites
5. Aphids
6. Thrips

KEY MANAGEMENT:
- Tuta absoluta: Spinosad (Tracer 480 SC, 150 ml/ha)
 + pheromone mass trapping; earthing-up 2-3 times
- Late Blight: Ridomil MZ Gold + resistant varieties
- Early Blight: Pseudomonas fluorescens Pfsa31 biocontrol
- General: Mancozeb, Copper Plus WP, crop rotation,
 staking, mulching, resistant varieties from EIAR

41. Ethiopian Institute of Agricultural Research (EIAR).
 "Onion Thrips (Thrips tabaci) Management Trials —
 Central Rift Valley." Establishes 5-10 thrips/plant

42. Bock K.R. (1964). "Purple blotch (Alternaria porri) of
 onion in Kenya." Annals of Applied Biology 54:303-313.
 [FOUNDATIONAL East African epidemiology reference]
 Established temperature/humidity/leaf wetness triggers:
 21-30°C + RH>75% + leaf wetness 8+ hr = disease.

CROP CONTEXT:
- Critical Ethiopian vegetable crop and export commodity
- Common varieties: Bombay Red, Adama Red, improved EIAR
 releases
- Main production zones: Central Rift Valley (Meki, Ziway,
 Adama), Amhara (Fogera Plains, Dera, Libo Kemkem),
 Southern Ethiopia (Arba Minch)
- Fertilizer recommendation: 100 kg/ha NPS + 100 kg/ha UREA

KEY DISEASES:
1. Purple Blotch (Alternaria porri) — MOST IMPORTANT, up to
 100% prevalence in some Fogera areas
2. Downy Mildew (Peronospora destructor) — up to 80% loss
3. Stemphylium Leaf Blight (S. vesicarium) — purple blotch
 complex co-pathogen
4. White Rot (Sclerotium cepivorum)
5. Basal Rot (Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. cepae)
6. Neck Rot (postharvest)

KEY PEST:
- Onion Thrips (Thrips tabaci) — MOST COMMON, vectors
 viruses, threshold 5-10/plant

KEY MANAGEMENT:
- Purple Blotch: Tebuconazole (Natura 250 EW) or
 Difenoconazole (Diprocon 33 EC), 2-3 sprays
- Cultural (low-input): early transplanting (Dec),
 plant after cereals, frequent plowing (>4×),
 smaller fields ≤0.25 ha
- Thrips: Neem, Datura (jimson weed), Beauveria
 bassiana — rotate to avoid resistance
